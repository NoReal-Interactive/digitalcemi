Discover the Cotton Cemí! A Journey through History, Science and Technology
In front of you is an interactive totem lets you explore a very special object: the cotton cemí.
But what is a cemí?
It's a sacred object used by some Indigenous peoples from the Caribbean islands. This one is made of cotton and is very rare. It has survived for centuries and traveled across the ocean to get here.
An All-Italian Investigation
In 2022, a group of Italian researchers started studying the cemí thanks to a project called E-RIHS. The work was done by the MAET – Museum of Anthropology and Ethnography at the University of Turin, together with scientists from the National Research Council (CNR). The researchers didn't open or damage the cemí. Instead, they used special non-invasive techniques to explore both the outside and the inside of the object. Here are some of the cool methods they used:
  • Photos taken in normal, infrared, and ultraviolet light to see hidden details.
  • A kind of "X-ray" scan to look inside.
  • Chemical tests to understand the materials, like the fibers and dyes.
All this helped them create a very detailed 3D digital model of the cemí — a perfect virtual copy of the real one!
Digital Cemí for Everyone
In 2024, a second project called TAZEBAO began. It was led by MAET again, this time with a company called No Real Interactive.
The goal was to make a digital twin of the cemí — a version that people all around the world can explore and enjoy.
How did they do it?
With the help of experts in 3D design, technology, storytelling, and history, they created:
  • real-time 3D model, easy to use on computers, tablets, phones, and even in virtual reality.
  • Videos, images, and texts to tell the cemí's story.
  • A phygital setup (physical and digital) that can be moved and shown in museums around the world.
  • An exciting VR experience where you can follow the cemí's journey: from its discovery to Europe, and through all the scientific studies.
